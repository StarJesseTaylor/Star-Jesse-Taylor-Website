## PHASE 1: THE COHORT (June 2026 launch)

**What it is:** A group coaching program that LA workshop attendees can join immediately after May 30. It's the bridge between "I had a great day in the room" and "I have integrated this into my life."

**Decision LOCKED IN (Mon May 4): 6 weeks Foundation + optional 6-week Advanced after.**

This is a TWO-TIER cohort structure. The full progression:

```
Tier 1: Foundation Cohort         6 weeks    Open to all post-LA
Tier 2: Advanced Cohort           6 weeks    Foundation graduates only
```

**Why 6+6 instead of one 10-week:**
- Lower commitment threshold for first sale (6 weeks feels doable, 10 weeks scares people away)
- Built-in upsell path (Foundation grads who want more pay again for Advanced)
- People who try Foundation and don't want more can opt out cleanly
- Two payment events vs one (better cash flow + revenue per customer)
- This is the same model Tony Robbins, Marie Forleo, and most premium personal-development brands use

## PHASE 2: THE TOUR (Q3-Q4 2026)

**The reframe — call it a tour, not a series of workshops.**

This is the biggest strategic move in this document. Read carefully.

When Mel Robbins, Brene Brown, Tony Robbins, Jay Shetty, or Gary Vaynerchuk does a multi-city workshop run, they don't call it "I'm doing workshops in some cities." They call it a TOUR. Why?

A tour:
- Creates a brandable moment (people remember a tour, not "events")
- Builds press momentum (every city stop is press-worthy)
- Creates urgency (limited dates, limited cities)
- Aggregates attention into one narrative
- Sells better (people buy into a tour story, not a one-off event)
- Creates a documentary-able arc (Caleb's footage becomes a thread, not snippets)
- Gives you a landing page that lives for 6 months instead of expiring after each event

**Working name options:**
- The Mental Fitness Tour 2026
- The Stop Reacting Tour
- The Foundation Tour
- The Out Of Your Head Tour

My pick: **"The Mental Fitness Tour"** — clean, evergreen-able for next year, ties to the book name.

**Tour cities Star mentioned:**
- Austin
- New York
- Miami
- Dallas
- New Orleans

Plus the LA workshop as Stop #1.

**My addition — strategic city ordering for momentum:**
1. Los Angeles (May 30) — already booked
2. Austin (July) — high creator/wellness density, easy travel from LA, lower cost
3. Miami (August) — strong wellness market, high spend per capita
4. New York (September) — biggest media market, biggest press opportunity, save for when momentum is built
5. Dallas (October) — corporate market entry point
6. New Orleans (November) — different vibe, expands market

This sequence builds press momentum through Austin → Miami → NYC. NYC stop is where you go for big media (Today Show, podcasts, Forbes). Don't waste NYC as the first stop before momentum builds.

**Tour mechanics:**

- Same workshop format as LA (full day, $97 GA / $347 VIP, 20-seat cap)
- Tour landing page: starjessetaylor.com/tour with all 6 cities
- "Bring Star to your city" form for any city not on the schedule (more on this in corporate section)
- Tour-wide email sequence (one per city announcement)
- Caleb travels for content capture
- Each stop generates: testimonial videos, behind-the-scenes content, attendee transformation stories, podcast/press appearances

**Tour budget per city (estimate):**

Hotel (2 nights × 2 people):       $400-800
Misc (food, supplies):             $300

## PHASE 3: THE CORPORATE TRACK (parallel to tour)

**This is where the actual money is.**

A Fortune 500 company paying for a half-day mental fitness training for their executive team is one engagement that can equal 50 public workshops in revenue. This deserves dedicated focus, not a footnote on the tour pages.

**What companies actually buy:**

Companies don't buy "mental health for employees." They buy:
- Reduced stress → reduced burnout → reduced turnover (HR/retention)
- Better focus → higher productivity (operations)
- Less reactivity in leadership → better decision-making (executives)
- Wellness/mental health benefits to attract top talent (employer brand)

**Star's offer to corporate clients:**

Three tiers:

```
TIER 1: Corporate Half-Day Workshop
Audience: Mid-level managers and high-performers (15-50 people)
Format: 4-hour live in-person session
Pricing: $7,500 - $15,000

TIER 2: Executive Full-Day Workshop
Audience: C-suite, founders, senior leaders (10-25 people)
Format: Full day, smaller intimate setting, more 1-on-1 hot seats
Pricing: $25,000 - $50,000

TIER 3: Annual Mental Fitness Partnership
Audience: Companies that want ongoing program (50-500 people)
Format: Quarterly workshops + monthly Zoom + Skool community for the team
Pricing: $50,000 - $250,000/year retainer
```

**Targeting strategy:**

- Wellness-forward tech companies (mid-size, 100-500 employees, where founders are progressive)
- VC-backed startups (founders care about team mental health)
- Marketing/creative agencies (high-burnout industries, where retention matters)
- Wellness brands (potential cross-promo)
- Female-founded businesses (higher openness to mental fitness investment)

**How leads come in:**

- "Bring Star to your company" form on website (I'll add this)
- Direct outbound from Star to founders he respects (warm intro through his network)
- Press tour creates inbound (NYC stop in particular)
- Each public tour stop generates corporate inquiries (always announce: "If your company would benefit, here's how to inquire")

**The "Bring Star to your city OR company" form:**

Create one form that handles both:
- City request (group of friends wanting Star to come) — routes to public tour planning
- Company request (employer wanting to hire Star) — routes to corporate sales conversation

Both feed into AC, tagged differently, both get follow-up sequences.

## TEN BIGGER IDEAS (read these — some of these are bigger than what you wrote)

### 1. The "Mental Fitness Manifesto" landing page

Create one page that sets the philosophical foundation for everything. Not a sales page — a manifesto. "Here is what mental fitness is. Here is why we are training the wrong things. Here is what changes when you train this." Feeds every other piece of marketing. Becomes the page you link to in every podcast appearance.

### 2. A book launch retrigger

Your book sold ~X copies (don't know your number). The book is your highest-trust asset. Re-launch it as part of the tour announcement. New foreword. Hardcover version. Audible. Sell it as part of every tour ticket. The book is undermarketed.

### 3. Sponsorship layer for the tour

Wellness brands (Calm, Headspace, Athletic Greens, BetterHelp), supplement companies (Magnesium, Lion's Mane), and mental health apps would pay $5K-25K to sponsor a tour stop. New revenue stream that doesn't compete with ticket sales. They get logo on event materials, mention in opening, sample table.

### 4. Recorded retreat/masterclass product

Even at $97 ticket price for the workshops, the recordings could be sold as a $497 self-paced masterclass to people who couldn't attend. Multiplier on each event's revenue. One workshop = $5K live revenue + potentially $30K+ in masterclass sales over 12 months.

### 5. The Documentary

Caleb is not just a content guy. He's a documentarian. Frame the entire 2026 tour as a documentary in the making. Year-end deliverable: a 60-90 minute film about what happens when you teach this work in person across America. Distributable on YouTube (free, builds audience), on a paid platform (Vimeo OnDemand, $9.99 rental), or as a bonus to The Practice members. The film becomes evergreen marketing for tour 2027.

### 6. Strategic co-stars at each city stop

At every tour city, invite a local creator/coach/influencer to attend free or even speak briefly (10 min slot). Their endorsement after the day becomes content. Their audience finds out about you. Cross-promo built into every event without you doing extra work.

### 7. The "Bring Star to your CITY or COMPANY" form

One form, two paths. People who want a city visit get tour planning follow-up. Companies get sales conversation. Both feed momentum and create demand signal Star can use to plan tour cities (don't book Dallas if no one in Dallas asked — but if you got 80 city requests from Atlanta, that's data).

### 8. The Founder Mastermind / Annual Mastermind

Above the Annual Corporate Partnership tier, there's a personal version: 8-12 founders/executives who pay $25K-50K each per year for monthly group calls + 2 in-person retreats with Star. Cap at 12 people. This is the highest-margin offer in the entire stack and where ultra-premium positioning gets built. Mel Robbins, Marie Forleo, Tim Ferriss all have this layer in their business.

### 9. Press tour BEFORE the public tour

**What "press tour" means in YOUR space (not Hollywood):**

In the personal development / wellness / business space, "press tour" doesn't mean walking red carpets. It means going on as many podcasts and shows as possible in a focused window. Specifically:

- 25-30 podcasts in 30-45 days
- Maybe 2-3 local TV/radio appearances if relevant
- IG Live cross-overs with other creators
- YouTube guest appearances on bigger channels in your niche

For someone in Star's space, **it's almost ALL podcasts.** That's 30 hours of audio interviews from his home setup, mostly via Riverside or Zoom. Not glamorous. Just volume + focus.

**Examples of personal-development press tours that worked:**
- Mel Robbins did 100+ podcasts when "The Mel Robbins Podcast" launched
- James Clear did massive podcast circuit for "Atomic Habits" launch (cited as a major reason it became #1 NYT bestseller)
- Tim Ferriss does press tours when each book drops
- Jay Shetty does press tours quarterly

**Why it works:** Listeners hearing Star on multiple shows in the same month creates a "this guy is everywhere" effect. That perception drives traffic + tour ticket sales much more efficiently than ads.

**How to execute:**
1. Build a list of 100 target podcasts (use ListenNotes, podcastbrowser, podchaser)
2. Pitch each via DM/email with a specific topic angle
3. Goal: 30 yes responses
4. Record 5-7 per week
5. Each release = a tour ticket sale push from that podcast's audience

I can help build the podcast outreach list and write the pitch template when we're ready.

### 10. The "What's Your Mental Algorithm?" diagnostic / quiz

A 60-second quiz on your homepage that diagnoses someone's brain algorithm pattern (fight, flee, fix, freeze, ruminate, etc.). At the end, they get a personalized result + an email sequence + a soft pitch to the right offer for them. Quiz funnels are the single highest-converting form of cold-traffic capture for personal development brands. This + the existing quiz funnel could double your leads.

### 11. Book yourself onto OTHER people's events (the speaker circuit)

This is huge and Star explicitly asked about it. Instead of (or in addition to) building your own tour, pitch yourself to events that already exist and already have audiences. They pay you to speak. You don't pay venue, marketing, or promotion. They handle everything. Caleb captures content.

**Types of existing events to target:**

**A) Conferences (wellness, mental health, personal development):**
- Wisdom 2.0 (mindfulness/tech crossover)
- SXSW Wellness track
- ALT Summit (creator/wellness)
- MindValley events
- Mind Body Spirit Festival
- Tony Robbins business events (when he books outside speakers)
- Women's wellness conferences (Belong Con, etc.)

**B) Corporate conferences (where companies bring outside speakers):**
- Inbound (Hubspot's annual)
- SaaStr Annual
- Forbes Women's Summit
- Fortune's Most Powerful Women
- Industry-specific: tech, finance, creative, etc.

**C) Industry trade shows:**
- APA conventions (psychology)
- ASCA (counselors)
- Wellness expos in major cities

**D) Online summits:**
- These are virtual events with 20-50 speakers each
- Lower fee but broader reach
- Examples: MindShare summits, Mindvalley summits, podcast networks running annual summits

**E) Festival speaking slots:**
- Wanderlust (yoga/wellness festival)
- Lightning in a Bottle
- Even Burning Man has speaking slots

**How to find them:**
- speakerhub.com (database of events looking for speakers)
- speakerwiki.com
- Pitch directly to event organizers via LinkedIn/email
- Sign with a speaker bureau (long-term play — see below)

**How to pitch:**

You need ONE speaker one-pager that includes:
- Headshot
- Bio (2-3 sentences)
- 3 talk topics (titles + 2-sentence descriptions each)
- Demo reel (90-second video of you speaking — you have 6 yrs of online speaking, plenty of footage)
- Audience reach numbers (1B+ views, 1M+ followers)
- Past speaking experience (use your virtual events / TikTok lives / podcasts)
- Booking contact email

Send this via DM to event organizers, or via their "submit a speaker" form on their website.

**3 talk topics I'd suggest:**
1. "The Algorithm In Your Brain — Why Your Patterns Repeat And How To Actually Change Them" (general audience)
2. "Mental Fitness For High-Performance Teams — Reducing Reactivity, Increasing Focus" (corporate audience)
3. "What 18 Years Of Self-Coaching Taught Me About Anxiety That No Therapist Did" (vulnerable / story-led)

**Speaker bureaus (long-term, after 5-10 paid speaking gigs):**

These take 25-30% of your fee but bring premium gigs at $5K-25K per talk:
- Harry Walker Agency (top tier, hard to get into)
- WSB (Washington Speakers Bureau)
- BigSpeak
- AAE Speakers Bureau
- All American Speakers
- Eagles Talent

Sign with one when you have 10+ paid speaking gigs as social proof. They handle bookings, you focus on showing up.

## ONE BIG IDEA TO PRIORITIZE OVER ALL OTHERS

If you read nothing else in this document, read this:

**Stop thinking of yourself as a coach who does workshops. Start thinking of yourself as the founder of a Mental Fitness movement.**

The difference is everything. A coach who does workshops is a service business. A founder of a movement is a leader who has multiple revenue streams (book, tour, cohort, corporate, retreats, masterminds, sponsorships, content, eventually app, eventually licensed program).

The right name on the homepage is not "Star Jesse Taylor, Coach." It's something like "Star Jesse Taylor — The Mental Fitness Movement" or "Star Jesse Taylor — Founder, Mental Fitness."

This is the framing shift that takes you from $500K/year coach to $5M/year movement founder over the next 24 months.
