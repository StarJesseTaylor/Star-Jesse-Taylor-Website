DESIGN GUIDELINES (apply across all slides)
----------------------------------------------------------------

- Format: 1080 x 1080 (Instagram square)
- Background: white or off-white (clean, modern)
- Accent color: cobalt blue (your brand color #1B6CA8)
- Font: Bold sans-serif (Inter, Montserrat, or Canva default)
- Headline size: HUGE (60-80pt) so it's readable on small phone screens
- Use page numbers in corner (1/8, 2/8, etc.) so people know to swipe
- Last slide: include "@starjessetaylor" handle and link-in-bio reminder

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 1: THE HOOK
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: Mock-up of a phone screen showing the Instagram or TikTok feed.
The phone is the focal point. Add a small brain emoji or icon
overlay where you'd see videos.

HEADLINE (large, centered, bold):
"Your brain has an algorithm.
(Just like TikTok.)"

BOTTOM TEXT (small):
"Swipe →"

PURPOSE: Stop the scroll with curiosity. The TikTok analogy is
familiar to every viewer.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 2: THE REFRAME
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: A clock or calendar with a subtle line crossed through "DAY"
and replaced with "MOMENT". Or a simple typographic treatment.

HEADLINE (large, centered):
"You don't have a bad day.

You have a bad moment."

SMALL TEXT BELOW:
"And bad moments can be changed."

PURPOSE: Pattern interrupt. People expect mental health content to
validate their bad day. You reframe instantly.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 3: THE CORE INSIGHT
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: Brain illustration with arrows pointing toward it.
Caption underneath the brain image.

HEADLINE (large):
"Whatever you give attention to grows."

SMALL TEXT BELOW:
"Your brain learns from what you watch.
Just like the algorithm learns from what you scroll."

PURPOSE: Set up the metaphor cleanly. This is the foundation of
the next 4 slides.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 4: THE PENGUIN PROBLEM
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: Phone screen with a penguin image (or several penguin
emoji 🐧) visible. You can use a Canva penguin illustration.

HEADLINE:
"Penguin videos = anxiety, intrusive thoughts, rumination."

BODY TEXT:
"You don't like them.
But you give them watch time.

Your brain thinks you love them."

PURPOSE: Name the problem in metaphor language. Make people
recognize their own pattern.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 5: THE TRAP
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: A circular arrow / loop graphic. Or a phone screen with
a comment field showing someone typing back at the algorithm.

HEADLINE:
"Trying to fix anxiety
gives anxiety more watch time."

BODY TEXT:
"It's like commenting on a penguin video
to make it stop showing you penguins.

The brain doesn't speak our language.
It only knows engagement."

PURPOSE: This is the moment of recognition. People have been doing
the wrong thing thinking it was the right thing.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 6: THE SHIFT
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: Phone screen with elephant imagery. Or split screen with
penguin on left (faded) and elephant on right (bright).

HEADLINE:
"Switch to elephant videos.

Your valued actions."

BODY TEXT:
"Walking. Eating. Working.
Being fully present in your life.

Moment to moment.
That is what rewires the brain."

PURPOSE: The solution. Frame it as a SWITCH, not a fight.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 7: THE PRACTICE
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: A simple icon or photo representing a daily action
(brushing teeth, walking, eating). Clean, minimal.

HEADLINE:
"Use the No Brain Method."

BODY TEXT (centered, larger):
"No brain, we are walking right now.
No brain, we are eating right now.
No brain, we are working right now."

SMALLER TEXT BELOW:
"When you say 'no brain,' you separate yourself
from the pattern. You are not the pattern.
You are the one watching it."

PURPOSE: Give them an actual tool. They walk away with something
to do tomorrow morning.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 8: THE CTA
----------------------------------------------------------------

VISUAL: A clean text card. No clutter. The date and location
should be the largest text.

HEADLINE (large):
"May 30. Los Angeles.

We do this work in person."

BODY TEXT:
"Full day intensive workshop.
20 seats only.

Link in bio for details."

BOTTOM:
"@starjessetaylor"

PURPOSE: Soft, confident CTA. Doesn't scream. Just states the
opportunity.
