THUMBNAIL STRATEGY
----------------------------------------------------------------

YOUR INSTINCT IS RIGHT. Use a whiteboard screenshot from the
video itself. Reasons:
1. Vertical video + whiteboard screenshot = consistent expectations.
   When the thumbnail matches the video, watch time goes up because
   viewers do not feel bait-and-switched.
2. Polished thumbnails on raw teaching videos can actually hurt
   trust with your audience. They want the teacher, not the brand.
3. The whiteboard already does the work. You drew the metaphor.
   The thumbnail just needs to make people curious about WHY.

HOW TO BUILD IT:
- Open the video, pause on the moment your whiteboard shows the
  brain + penguin + elephant clearest (probably mid-video).
- Screenshot the frame.
- Open Canva, choose YouTube Thumbnail (1280 x 720).
- Paste your screenshot as the background.
- Add ONE line of bold text overlay (cobalt blue or red text on
  white background box for max contrast on small phone screens).

CURIOSITY TEXT OPTIONS (pick one, or test 2 across the week):

GOLD TIER (highest curiosity, lowest cliche):
1. "It took me 18 years to figure this out"
   (Your instinct. Strong. Personal. Implies a payoff.)

2. "Why your brain keeps showing you anxiety"
   (Reframes the viewer's problem as something the brain is
   doing TO them. Creates the question: how do I stop it?)

3. "Stop trying to fix anxiety. You are feeding it."
   (Pattern interrupt. Says the thing nobody else says.
   Mirrors your subject line for Email 3.)

SOLID TIER (good but more familiar):
4. "Your brain has an algorithm"
   (Direct from the slide deck. Punchy.)

5. "The mistake that keeps you stuck"
   (Curiosity gap. Slightly more clickbait-feeling.)

6. "What 18 years taught me about anxiety"
   (Authority + curiosity. Less dramatic than #1.)

EXPERIMENTAL TIER (could pop, could flop):
7. "Your brain is the TikTok algorithm"
   (Bold claim. Some viewers will instantly want to know what
   you mean. Others will scroll past thinking they get it.)

8. "I was wrong about anxiety for 18 years"
   (Vulnerability hook. Strong on YouTube specifically because
   confession-style thumbnails outperform claim-style.)

MY PICK FOR YOU: #1 ("It took me 18 years to figure this out")
- It is your voice
- It promises a payoff
- It does not feel like marketing
- It works on a whiteboard screenshot without competing with the drawing

If you want to test 2: pair #1 and #3.

THUMBNAIL TEXT STYLING:
- Font: Bold sans-serif (Inter Bold, Montserrat Black, or Anton)
- Size: at least 80pt so it reads on a phone screen
- Color: White text inside a cobalt blue (#1B6CA8) or red rectangle
  OR black text on a yellow rectangle (highest contrast on YouTube)
- Place text in upper-left or lower-third, NOT covering your drawing
- Optional: a small arrow or circle drawn around the brain on the
  whiteboard, to draw the eye

After 18 years of obsessively seeking, this is the only practice that rewired my brain.

I tried therapy. I read every self-help book. I tried supplements, retreats, breathwork, journaling, every framework you can name. None of it actually changed how my brain worked.

Then I figured out one thing: your brain is running an algorithm.

Whatever you give attention to, your brain assumes you love. Even if you hate it. Even if you are TRYING to fix it. The intrusive thoughts, the anxiety, the rumination, your brain treats every reaction as a vote to give you more of the same.

In this video I break down:

→ Why "having a bad day" is the wrong frame, and what to use instead
→ How your brain works exactly like the TikTok algorithm
→ The "penguin video" problem (and why most mental health advice makes it worse)
→ The shift from reactive brain to proactive brain
→ The No Brain Method, a tool you can use tomorrow morning

This is the same teaching I do with my 1-on-1 clients, my group coaching, and at my live workshops.
