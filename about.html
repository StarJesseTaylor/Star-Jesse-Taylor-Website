About Star

I didn't wait for someone to save me. I saved myself. Then I learned how to help you do the same.

From Frankfurt to Los Angeles

I grew up in Frankfurt, Germany. At some point I made a decision that changed everything: I was moving to the United States. Alone. No family here. No safety net. No one to call if things went sideways. Just me, showing up in a new country and building a life from the ground up. I had to figure out how to survive, how to belong, and how to build something real, all at the same time, with no one who had done it before me to ask.

What Survival Taught Me

To make it work, I took on whatever I could find. I drove Uber and Lyft. And for four years, I worked as a children's entertainer, performing for and working with kids from ages 4 to 16. Working with kids opened my heart in a way I did not expect. Children see the world with so few limitations. Their creativity, their honesty, and their unfiltered way of experiencing emotion taught me things no textbook could. That time expanded my own creativity and compassion and shaped how I see and coach people today. I stopped doing that work in 2020. For the past six years I have worked exclusively as a coach, speaker, podcast guest, author, and social media creator.

Theater and Emotional Presence

I also had a life in theater. I trained with the Steppenwolf Theater Company, one of the most respected theater companies in the world. I appeared in a TV show, worked on music videos and short films, and spent years on stage. What I understand now is that I was already practicing emotional fitness long before I had a name for it. Every improv game, every scene, every moment of jumping on stage in front of an audience taught me how to be fully present, how to feel things completely, and how to move through fear in real time without stopping. Theater did not teach me to perform my emotions. It taught me to work with them.

Years of Deep Study

From a very young age I knew I wanted to understand how life worked at the deepest level. Not the surface version. The actual mechanics. So I studied everything I could find. I read Joseph Murphy's The Power of Your Subconscious Mind and began understanding how the mind creates reality. I went deep into Abraham Hicks and the Law of Attraction, attending over 10 Abraham Hicks workshops and sitting in the hot seat 5 times to ask questions directly, several of which are on YouTube. I read The Secret by Rhonda Byrne. I practiced Eckhart Tolle's teachings on present moment awareness from The Power of Now for years. I attended Bashar workshops and spoke directly with Bashar at least three times, practicing his teaching of following your highest excitement with no insistence on the outcome. I am a deep student of Michael Singer's work, including The Untethered Soul and The Surrender Experiment, and have practiced surrender as a daily discipline ever since. My first experience with transformational seminars was the Landmark Forum, where I completed both the regular course and the advanced course.

If Any of This Resonates With You

I share all of this because I want you to understand what you are actually dealing with here. This is not someone who read a few books and became a coach. This is someone who spent years in the real world, studied every major framework for human transformation, practiced all of it on himself, moved to a new country completely alone, built a life from nothing, and came out the other side with something real to teach. I did not find a coach who could help me with everything I was going through. So I became my own.

What makes this work different is the intention behind it. Most coaches create dependency. My entire approach is built around the opposite. I teach people how to become their own coach, their own authority. To trust their instincts. To develop a real connection with a higher intelligence that is available to all of us. The goal of every session, every cohort, and every book is that you need me less over time, not more. The goal is that you graduate.

If you feel it, trust that. Apply below.

The Framework

The Three Pillars of Emotional Fitness

These are some of the foundational principles inside the Emotional Fitness framework. A glimpse into the insights, tools, and shifts people experience when doing this work.

🌱

The Value Garden

Your life is a garden with seven areas: physical fitness, relationships, creative expression, business, relaxation, fun, and romantic connection. Most people wait for the brain to feel ready before they grow it. Emotional fitness teaches the truth: your garden doesn't care how you feel. It only needs to be watered. Valued actions are the water.

🧠

From Reactive to Proactive

The brain runs an emotional addictive engine, pulling you into rumination, compulsions, and avoidance to protect you from discomfort. Emotional fitness teaches you to shift your GPS from reacting to feelings to acting on values. Tools like the No Brain Method give you a moment-to-moment practice for making that shift, even in the middle of anxiety or fear.

✨

The Daily Practice

Emotional fitness is built the same way physical fitness is, through daily repetition. That means silent meditation to build the capacity to hold uncomfortable emotions, thankfulness journaling to shift your morning from reactive to positive, and scheduling your valued actions the night before so the brain cannot fill that space with compulsions. The practice compounds. So does the strength.
