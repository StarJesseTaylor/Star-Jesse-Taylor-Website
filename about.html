From Frankfurt to Los Angeles

I grew up in Frankfurt, Germany and moved to the United States completely alone with no family and no safety net. I had to independently find my own way in a new country from scratch.

What Survival Taught Me

To survive I drove Uber and Lyft and worked as a children's entertainer for four years with kids ages 4 to 16. Those years taught me more about human psychology than almost anything else. Children do not hide their emotions. They show you exactly what is happening inside them.

Working with kids opened my heart in a way I did not expect. Children see the world with so few limitations. Their creativity, their honesty, and their unfiltered way of experiencing emotion taught me things no textbook could. That time expanded my own creativity and compassion and shaped how I see and coach people today.

Star stopped driving Uber and working with kids in 2020. For the past six years he has worked exclusively as a coach, speaker, podcast guest, author, and social media creator.

Theater and Emotional Presence

I have a theater background and trained with the Steppenwolf Theater Company. I appeared in a TV show and worked on music videos and short films. Theater taught me emotional presence before I had a name for it. Every scene, every moment on stage in front of an audience taught me how to be fully present, how to feel things completely, and how to move through fear in real time without stopping.

The Moment That Changed Everything

I was living in Toronto working at a vegan restaurant. I got fired after four weeks. With my last paycheck I knew I had to get to Los Angeles. I could not find a ticket I could afford. A friend took me to a flight center and the woman there showed me a price that was exactly the amount of my last paycheck. Not close. Exactly. I flew to Los Angeles. That is not coincidence. That is what happens when you follow your heart with no insistence on the outcome. That moment proved to me that surrender is not a philosophy. It is a technology.

Years of Deep Study

From a very young age I studied spirituality and self help intensely. I read Joseph Murphy and The Power of Your Subconscious Mind. I studied Abraham Hicks deeply, attending over 10 workshops and sitting in the hot seat 5 times, several of which are on YouTube. I studied Eckhart Tolle and practiced present moment awareness for years. I attended Bashar workshops and spoke with Bashar directly at least 3 times, practicing following your excitement with no insistence on the outcome. I am a deep student of Michael Singer's work including The Untethered Soul and The Surrender Experiment. My first transformational seminar was the Landmark Forum where I completed both the regular and advanced courses.

This Is For You If

As you can see this is not someone who read a few books and became a coach. This is someone who spent years in the real world, studied every major framework for human transformation, moved to a new country alone, followed his heart to Los Angeles with his last paycheck, and came out the other side with something real to teach. I did not find a coach who could truly help me. So I became my own. That is why I can help you.

Practiced It vs Studied It

Most Therapists Read About It.
I Practiced It for 18 Years.

Most of the mental health industry is built on people who learned about the brain in a classroom. They have the degree. They have the textbook. Most of them never actually changed their own brain.

That's like a gym trainer who only read books about lifting and never put a single weight on the bar. They can quote the muscle groups. They can't build them. They have no muscle memory. They don't know what it feels like when your body hits the wall and you push through. So how would they teach you to do it?

Or a tennis coach who studied the game on paper but never picked up a racket. They can describe the grip. They can't feel the swing. They've never been in a real match with the score tight and their hands shaking. That's most mental health professionals.

I spent 18 years practicing. Not just reading. I changed my own brain and built Emotional Fitness because I needed something that actually worked, and what was on the shelf did not. The framework is what made me a better listener, a better friend, a better son, more loving, more present, more successful in my work and my business. Every tool I give you is something I tested in my own life first.

You don't need more letters after a name. You need someone who practiced what they teach and built a way through.

The Approach

Why My Approach Is Different

My approach is different from most coaches and therapists and here is why.

Most approaches to anxiety, OCD, intrusive thoughts, and mental health go extremely specific. ERP for OCD. CBT for anxiety. ACT for intrusive thoughts. And those tools have value. I teach them too.

But here is what nobody talks about.

People with these struggles love to chase the specific solution. The magical key. The one technique that will finally fix everything. And the mental health industry feeds that hunger because specific sells. It gives people the emotional entertainment of feeling like they are finally on the right track.

The problem is that you are not just a set of symptoms. You are a whole human being. And a whole human being cannot be fixed by only working on one thing.

If your car is not running you do not only fix the tires. You look at the engine, the fuel, the structure, the whole system. Fix only the tires and the car still does not run.

If you have a foot injury you do not only treat the foot. You strengthen your entire body, build muscle, improve your overall fitness so the injury heals faster and never comes back.

Your mind works the same way.

What I teach is different.

Yes you get the specific tools. The ACT-based defusion techniques for intrusive thoughts. The ERP framework for OCD. The practical methods for anxiety and panic. The tools for depersonalization and derealization. The strategies for procrastination and taking action.

But you also get the holistic foundation that makes all of those tools actually work. Spirituality and surrender. Meditation and present moment awareness. Physical fitness and how it directly impacts your mental state. Building a real social life and genuine connection. Structuring your days around valued actions. Developing a relationship with God or a higher intelligence. Getting out of your head and into your life.

Without the holistic foundation the specific tools are like fixing the tires on a car with no engine. They help a little but they do not get you moving.

With the holistic foundation the specific tools become exponentially more powerful because your whole system is charged up and ready to use them.

This is why my clients get results that therapy alone never delivered. Not because the specific tools are better. But because for the first time they are working on the whole car, not just the tires.

The Framework

The Three Pillars of Emotional Fitness

These are some of the foundational principles inside the Emotional Fitness framework. A glimpse into the insights, tools, and shifts people experience when doing this work.

🌱

The Value Garden

Your life is a garden with seven areas: physical fitness, relationships, creative expression, business, relaxation, fun, and romantic connection. Most people wait for the brain to feel ready before they grow it. Emotional fitness teaches the truth: your garden doesn't care how you feel. It only needs to be watered. Valued actions are the water.

🧠

From Reactive to Proactive

The brain runs an emotional addictive engine, pulling you into rumination, compulsions, and avoidance to protect you from discomfort. Emotional fitness teaches you to shift your GPS from reacting to feelings to acting on values. Tools like the No Brain Method give you a moment-to-moment practice for making that shift, even in the middle of anxiety or fear.

✨

The Daily Practice

Emotional fitness is built the same way physical fitness is, through daily repetition. That means silent meditation to build the capacity to hold uncomfortable emotions, thankfulness journaling to shift your morning from reactive to positive, and scheduling your valued actions the night before so the brain cannot fill that space with compulsions. The practice compounds. So does the strength.
